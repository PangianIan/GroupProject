    Early Life and Works

    Kirkhope was born in Edinburgh, Scotland, but grew up in Knaresborough, New Yorkshire. He started his love of music from an early age, learning guitar by eleven, avidly listening to musicians like Frank Sinatra, and being classically trained in the trumpet. He attended the Royal Norther College of Music where he was a part of many bands. After college in 1995, Kirkhope joined Rare to compose for their games.

    Rare and Later Works

    While at Rare, Kirkhope composed for many of their big games. These include Goldeneye, Banjo Kazooie, and Donkey Kong 64. He also created the soundtrack for Viva Pinata, which ended upgetting him nominated a BAFTA award for his score. In 2008, he moved to Baltimore, Maryland where he worked on Big Huge Games' Kingdom of Amalur. This also got him nominated for several awards for the soundtrack. Eventually, however, he decided to settle down in Los Angeles, California. There, he was nominated for even more awards for Civilization: Beyond Earth. He is also the only Western composer who was asked by Nintendo to work on Mario + Rabbids Sparks of Hope.
