    • Yoko Shimomura

    Early Life

    Shimomura was born in Hyogo, Japan. She started learning piano and composing music around the age of five. She then went to the Osaka College for Music and graduated as a piano major in 1988. After thinking of being a piano instructor, she decided to instead apply to video game companies due to her enjoyment of video games. She was eventually offered a job at Capcom and accepted it (to the dismay of her parents and instructors who say it as a lesser form of music).

    Early Career in Video Games

    At Capcom, Shimomura composed for over sixteen games like Street Fighter II and Final Fight. Many of these games even released albums of the soundtracks that sold well, including ones that were solely her work. She also joined the band at Capcom, Alpha Lyla, who she performed with live several times.

    In 1993, however, she left Capcom to join Square, stating that the "classical style" of Square fit her style more than Capcom. This is because she was mostly constricted to the arcade section of Capcom instead of console games. To begin at Square, she made the score for Live A Live. She also created the scores to many of their big projects like Super Mario RPG and Front Mission, which she worked on concurrently.

    Later Works

    After the introduction of the PlayStation, Shimomura made a few more scores for fairly high-profile games. These include ones such as Parasite Eve and Legend of Mana (the later of which Shimomura states was the best representation of her work). Meanwhile, Parasite eve was the first work of her to include a vocal song, which would become very popular in her later work. Eventually, she began to work on what would be one of the biggest turning points of her career: the score for Kingdom Hearts. Kingdom Hearts sold extremely well and many of the praises were about the soundtrack itself. It was also the last work Shimomura did working under Square, as she left on maternity leave and decided to do freelance work afterwards. Since then, she has worked on every Kingdom Hearts soundtrack, the Mario and Luigi rpgs, and Final Fantasy XV. Overall, she has become one of the more recognizable video game composers in history.
