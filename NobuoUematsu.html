    Early Works

    Nobuo Uematsu is a Japanese composer from the Kochi Prefecture. While he was at Kanagawa University studying English, he played music in several bands and composed minor jingles. He never had any formal training, but he was self-taught in the piano and learned to write music. Eventually, he was approached by his friend Hironobu Sakaguchi and asked if he would like to compose music for the game Cruise Chaser Blassty. Nobuo Uematsu then continued to help write music for Square, the company Sakaguchi worked at. However, none of the games he composed for were extremely successful until FInal Fantasy.

    Final Fantasy and Square

    After Final Fantasy became a commercial hit, Uematsu began to compose a lot of music for Square. To begin with, he composed for pretty much every Final Fantasy game after that, which had become an incredibly popular series of the genre. In addition, he helped compose the music for other games by Square, like Chrono Trigger. After the success of his music in the games, Uematsu even started his own band called The Black Mages with some of his coworkers: Kenichiro Fukui, Tsuyoshi Sekito, Keiji Kawamori, Arata Hanyuda, and Michio Okamiya. This band, as the name implies, mainly performed songs from Final Fantasy games, but did release a few new titles like "Blue Blast - Winning the Rainbow."

    Dog Ear Records and Other Works

    After he decided his time working directly for Square was over, Uematsu created Dog Ear Records as his own record and studio and production company. Uematsu has stated the main reason for this split was simply due to the moving of Square Enix's headquarters, which he had no intention of doing. After this, while still working with Square Enix, Uematsu began working for other studios as well like Mistwalker which was made by his Sakaguchi. Uematsu even wrote songs for some Nintendo games like the main theme of Super Smash Bros Brawl. Many of Uematsu's works even went on to be inducted in several Halls of Fame. However, Uematsu began a hiatus in 2018 due to health concerns and the stress of work, which had even left him hospitalized.
