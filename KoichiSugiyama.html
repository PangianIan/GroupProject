    Early Works

    Sugiyama started by working for Nippon Cultural Broadcasting as a reporter and entertainer. Eventually, he became a director of Fuji TV, but left in 1965 to focus on music composition. For about two decades after, he began to compose for commercials, shows, and other forms of media. Some notable ones are Cyborg 009 and his assistance on Godzilla vs. Hedorah. Overall, this previous work helped make him a celebrity.

    Enix and Death

    In the 1980's, Sugiyama sent a letter to Enix about a shogi game they had made. Suprised to have recieved a letter from someone of Sugiyama's fame, they asked him to compose for them given his knowledge of their games. Sugiyama accepted, and the first game he composed for them was Wingman 2. However, much more well known, he later composed for Dragon Quest, where his musical score was well known for being the first to use a live orchestra to record it and was seen as revolutionary. Sugiyama even released a CD based purely on the games where he had an orchestra playing. This was done with the help of the Tokyo Strings Ensemble. He has since composed for every single Dragon Quest game up to Dragon Quest XI. He also began to hold many events where the Tokyo Strings Ensemble and the London Philharmonic Orchestra would play songs he had composed, as well as those of seventeen others. For his work, Sugiyama was declared a Person of Cultural Merit in the country of Japan. Sugiyama died in 2021 from septic shock. Afterwords, a TV broadcast was done on Nippon TV to celebrate his life and work.

    Style

    Sugiyama is well known for his use of motifs throughout his work. As an example, every Dragon Quest game has practically identical versions of the Overture theme. It has also been said that his style was inspired by the Baroque and Classical periods of music. Sugiyama reportedly used drafts of stories he was given in order to determine the correct tone and feeling needed throughout the piece.
