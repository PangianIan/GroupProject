    Early Life and Works

    Koji Kondo is a Japanese composer from Nagoya, Japan. He started learning music in kindergarten when he learned to play the electric organ at the age of five. From there, he played marimba in a school band and the electric organ for a cover band that he was a member of. Despite all of this and going to Osaka University, Kondo never was classically trained in music. However, he was eventually mentored by Yukio Kaneoka on sound programming when he started working for Nintendo.

    Early Nintendo Works

    Koji Kondo's first work for Nintendo was on Punch-Out!! working on some of the game audio design. After his work on Devil World, Shigeru Miyamoto sought to work for Nintendo R&D4. After joining, Koji Kondo worked on the theme for Super Mario Bros, which would become his first majorly known work. After all, since then, the theme has been in most of the Mario games and in over 50 concerts. It was even the most popular ringtone at one point. After his work on the original Mario's music, his next work was for the original The Legend Of Zelda game. Here, he made 4 pieces of background music, the main overworld theme becoming another immensly successful piece.

    Proceeding Works

    Since then, Kondo has been one of the most notable names in video game musics. Kondo continued to work on Mario and Zelda soundtracks (who had both become incredibly successful series by this point), but also worked on other series like Yoshi's Island and Star Fox. While he has recently taken on more of an executive role, Kondo still composes music for many of these series.

    Interesting Facts

    • Koji Kondo often worked on many of his early game compositions on his own. The last composition he did this with was Ocarina of Time.
    • Kondo tried to incorporate elements of dance music into his music based on the ideas of Miyamoto about how game music should have music that was in sync with the game elements. Kondo believed this would give the games a feeling of motion you would get in the game.
    • Kondo's once said his favorite albums were Made in Japan and Brain Salad Surgery.
